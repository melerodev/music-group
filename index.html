Our History

Formed in 1983, Bon Jovi became one of the biggest rock bands in the world, known for anthems like "Livin' on a Prayer" and "It's My Life." With over 130 million records sold, they continue to shape the music industry. Their journey has been marked by countless awards, sold-out tours, and a legacy that spans generations. From their early days in New Jersey to becoming global icons, Bon Jovi's music resonates with fans worldwide, inspiring hope, love, and resilience.

Band Members

Jon Bon Jovi

Lead Vocals, Guitar

John Francis Bongiovi Jr. (born March 2, 1962), known professionally as Jon Bon Jovi, is an American singer, songwriter, guitarist, and actor. He is the founder and frontman of the rock band Bon Jovi, which was formed in 1983. Throughout his career, Bon Jovi has released 15 studio albums with his band and has sold over 130 million albums worldwide.

Beyond his music career, Jon has established the Jon Bon Jovi Soul Foundation in 2006, a nonprofit organization dedicated to combating hunger and homelessness in the United States.

Twitter Instagram Soul Foundation

David Bryan

Keyboards, Backing Vocals

David Bryan Rashbaum (born February 7, 1962), known professionally as David Bryan, is an American musician and songwriter, best known as the keyboard player for the rock band Bon Jovi. He is also a Tony Award-winning composer and lyricist for the Broadway musical "Memphis".

Bryan has released a solo album, "Lunar Eclipse" in 2000, and co-wrote the Broadway musical "Memphis", which won four Tony Awards in 2010, including Best Musical and Best Original Score.

Tico Torres

Drums

Hector Samuel Juan "Tico" Torres (born October 7, 1953) is an American musician, artist, and entrepreneur, best known as the drummer for the rock band Bon Jovi. Torres has been with the band since its inception in 1983.

Beyond his musical career, Torres is also an accomplished painter and has held several art exhibitions. He has also designed a line of baby clothes called "Rock Star Baby" which features his own artwork.

Instagram Rock Star Baby

Phil X

Lead Guitar

Theofilos Xenidis (born March 10, 1966), known professionally as Phil X, is a Greek-Canadian musician, songwriter, and producer. He officially joined Bon Jovi in 2016, replacing founding member Richie Sambora.

Before joining Bon Jovi, Phil X was a session guitarist who worked with numerous artists including Tommy Lee, Methods of Mayhem, and Alice Cooper. He also founded the band The Drills in 2003.

Twitter Instagram Official Website

Hugh McDonald

Bass

Hugh McDonald (born December 28, 1950) is an American musician who is best known as the bassist for the rock band Bon Jovi. Although he was not officially recognized as a member of Bon Jovi until 2016, McDonald has been playing bass for the band since 1994, replacing founding member Alec John Such.

Before joining Bon Jovi, McDonald was a session musician who played with various artists including Alice Cooper, Ringo Starr, and Cher. He also played bass on Jon Bon Jovi's 1990 solo album "Blaze of Glory".
